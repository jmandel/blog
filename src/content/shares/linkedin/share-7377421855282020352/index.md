Thrilled to announce the official publication of the HL7 FHIR Implementation Guide for Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)! 🎉 This new standard creates a secure, consistent path for continuous glucose monitoring data to flow from patient devices and apps directly into the Electronic Health Record (EHR). It supports a unified, longitudinal view that clinicians and patients can use together to make better, more collaborative care decisions. Details below...

Published Spec: https://lnkd.in/gEYgwqDj

This was a true community effort, launched by the Argonaut Project to build initial consensus and brought to maturity through the formal standards process at the HL7 Orders and Observations Work Group.

The success of this IG is a testament to the diverse group of stakeholders who came to the table. A huge thank you to the many organizations that contributed their expertise and resources, including: Abbott Medical Devices, Altera Digital Health, Apple, Darena Solutions, Dexcom, Duke University, Epic, Google, iCoDE, MedMij, MEDITECH, Medtronic, Microsoft, NCQA, Oracle, Redox, Rhapsody, Rimidi, Sensotrend Oy, and The Commons Project.

A special thanks to my co-editor Eric Haas, and key contributors Mikael Rinnetmäki, Brett Marquard, and Marti Velezis for their tireless efforts in bringing this to the finish line.

And a huge thank you to Mary Anne Patton (for her invaluable patient perspective), Tidepool, Sensotrend, and Rimidi for participating in the deep-dive interviews that were so crucial for shaping this project.

This standard is a bridge between CGM platforms and the clinical workflow, empowering patients and providers with shared, actionable insights. Onward!
